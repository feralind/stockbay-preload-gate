Can you make a profit?

Yes — with judgment and time. Careful ends ~$765,656 NW at D1000 with 0 late pays and Fair+/Excellent credit (personal 680 / business 850). Aggressive ends richer (~$2,239,430) but with nuked credit (989 late pays, both scores at 300) and collapsed bank facility — profit without credit health.

AFK ends underwater (−$1,817); payroll exceeds trading P&L (114%). Skill gap holds.

Are goals achievable?

Trusted Trader (REP 120) arrives early in this harness (D4–10) because green-day REP is inflated by synthetic P&L — treat REP timing as upper-bound, not player-realistic.

Careful unlocks Trading Floor ~D448 and AI ~D557; buys an estate after the mid-game trough. Aggressive still buys two estates on cash while credit is ruined (HELOC blocked at 300). Legend Desk only on aggressive (~D475). AFK never reaches estate NW gates.

How challenging is it?

The real challenge is the mid-game desk, not the first buy
Careful NW troughs around D300–400 (deeply negative, ~−$6k to −$10k) while staff payroll climbs. Trading Floor waits until ~D448 for cash. That slog teaches cost control — but still risks a long mid-game tunnel before recovery is visible on the HUD.

What do you learn when you play?

Days 1–5 (Teach)

Equity vs cash, size ≤25% (Sized right), simulated tape honesty, loan auto-pay ≠ free money. Graduation at Trusted Trader reframes the desk as judgment, not tutorial.

Days ~120–500 (Habits)

Patience paid / No chase chips reward process. Staff are a real cost center. Credit rebuild is slow; late pays permanently scar aggressive desks.

Late game (Identity)

Estates, vault, office, mega goals are flair/identity sinks. Perks should amplify judgment — not replace it. Clean-credit careful path is the authentic “I ran a firm” ending.
